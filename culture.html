Culture and traditions

  • Mexico has many traditions and culture
  • One of the most well known traditions that people do in Mexico is celebrate "dia de los muertos" which translate to Day of the Dead
  • They also have huge parties for girls who turn 15 years old called quinceañera that represents the girl transitiong into a women.
  • Mexicans have a love for dancing and one of the most popular dances is folklore dancing
  • The Day of the Dead is celebrated all across Mexico. Its a day where families honor their dead loved ones. They make an altar which they call an "ofrenda." They decorate the altars with pictures, foods, marigolds and candles
  • Quinceañera is a birthday celebrations for girls who turn 15 years old. It is a mark for the girl transitiong into womenhood.
  • The birthday celebration starts off with Mass, and a party right after that includes food, cherographed dances and formal entrances
  • Folklore dancing is also a huge part of Mexico's culture. These dances represent the rich history and identity of Mexico.
  • Girls wear colorful ruffled dresses with ribbons in their hair the boys wear dark linen shirts and pants and a sombrero
